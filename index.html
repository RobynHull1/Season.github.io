    There are four main seasons in the Northern and Southern Hemisphere:

  1. Spring
  2. Summer
  3. Autumn
  4. Winter
  5. Holidays

    The places on the Equator experience two major season:

  1. Rainy Season
  2. Sunny Season

Spring

Spring begins in life March and ends in June in the Southern hemisphere and on September to December. This time on the year brings about a new beginning. Trees and flowers bloom, animals emerge from sleep and the earth is starting life again.

Spring Equinox

Summer

Summer is the hottest season of the year. It sometimes creates droughts but it also helps animals become active.

Summer Solstice

Autumn

Autumn is the time of pumpkins. It's beautiful. Autumn lights just seem so fairy tale like. Autumn is when the weather begins to get cold and the leaves begin to fall.

Autunm Equinox

Winter

Winter is the end of all four seasons. It's when the animals go into hiding because the place is freezing. Whatever place of the Earth that is tilted away from the sun will experience winter.

Winter Solstice
